Case Study · One Question At 12:19

Everything that was specced shipped and works — I re-verified the survivors today: all 8 stores adapt with zero errors, every mutating route still answers stores are read-only, and the tombstones render as an archive strip, exactly as the audit demanded. What Chris's question exposed was not a build defect. Clicking a record — the way he actually reads a log entry or a key result — did nothing in six of seven record views. Confirmed in the round-1 code: HQInspector appears 4× in Board, 4× in Projects, and zero times in the five views this mission created or repointed.

Root Cause
The verification bar compressed at every rewrite — “click every control” became “have a look”, then “click through every page”, then “clicked all 8 tabs.” Every layer executed its own copy faithfully; the bar itself was lossy-rewritten, Chris's dictation included.
or001 named the mechanism — requirements die by summarization — on Friday's build chain. This run reproduces it on the management chain, in real time, 42 minutes before or001 was filed. Nobody defied the method: the builder did exactly what its stop point said, the chief did exactly what step 5 said. What no layer carried was the bar in Chris's own words: interact with every record the way Chris would. One question at 12:19 restored it, and the dead clicks surfaced in six minutes.

Playbook Deltas For The Method

The six steps survived contact — keep all of them. These eight bolt onto the method doc; each traces to a specific win or gap above.

Carry the bar verbatim

Write the verification bar once, in Chris's words — “click every control, open every record, read what you see” — as a named checklist in the method doc, and paste it verbatim into every brief, stop point, and verify prompt. It was paraphrased four times and died by the fourth. or001 proved checklists eclipse prose; this run proved paraphrase eats bars.

Chief's STEP 5 floor: one record per view

Real pointer clicks (eval-clicks lie about clickability), at the owner's viewport, one record opened in every view, and read what appears. The probe measured the cost: six minutes. 6 minutes buys what 92 didn't.

Events wake the chief; cron is the deadman

Builder “done” and questions go out as --interrupt to the chief's session; the cron survives only as a slow silence-detector. State moves out of cron prompts and into the mission row, ending the delete-recreate churn. Target: −25 min tick-wait per mission at this scale.

Wire-check 60 seconds after every spawn

learning_verify-wire-after-spawn already exists with evidence — promote it from a learning into the method doc and the spawn recipe. Delivery ≠ alive; two 500s cost 21% of this mission.

Re-baseline time to agent speed

When the “20–30 min read” lands in 2 minutes, every downstream estimate is off by an order of magnitude: halve the tick interval, drop “hours” language, and size verify effort as a ratio of observed build time, never a fixed hour.

Spec the click, not just the view

Every view row in a plan carries one more column: on record click → what. An empty cell is a decision that must be made before build, not after the owner asks. This is or001's “spec states, not screens” applied to interactions.

Silence the wolf

Watchdog suppresses “gone quiet” for agents with no open mission, and alert classes tied to a fixed bug retire with the fix. Twelve unactionable alerts per four-hour window is how the thirteenth, real one, gets skimmed past.

File the lesson while it stings

The moment a probe lands, the lesson goes into learnings.jsonl with the probe as its evidence ref — same turn as the apology. A lesson that lives only in chat is repeated; one that lives in the store is loaded into the next chief's onboarding.
