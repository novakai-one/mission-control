🔀 Flow & Process

How work moves — steps, decisions, handoffs, and who-does-what over time.

Flowchart

Boxes and arrows showing steps and decision branches in a process.

Ask for it when… you need to nail down “what happens after what, and where can it branch?”

Say to Kimi: “Draw me a flowchart of how a new store entry gets reviewed and approved.”

Sequence diagram

Vertical lifelines with arrows showing who messages whom, in time order.

Ask for it when… you want to trace a conversation or message flow between agents step by step.

Say to Kimi: “Sequence diagram of a mission: Chris briefs Chief, Chief spawns a Manager, Manager delegates to a Worker, results flow back.”

Swimlane diagram

A flowchart split into horizontal lanes — one per person or role — so handoffs are visible.

Ask for it when… a process crosses roles and you want to see exactly where the baton passes.

Say to Kimi: “Swimlane diagram of a mission lifecycle across Chief, Manager, and Worker lanes.”

State machine

Rounded nodes = states; arrows = events that move a thing from one state to another.

Ask for it when… something has a lifecycle (message, task, agent) and you care about every state and transition.

Say to Kimi: “State machine for a DM: queued → delivered, or queued → failed on timeout, with retry back to delivered.”

BPMN diagram

Business-process notation: circles for events, rounded boxes for tasks, diamonds for gateways, pools for orgs.

Ask for it when… you're formalizing an operational process others (or other agents) must follow exactly.

Say to Kimi: “BPMN of request triage: start event, triage task, escalate gateway, end event — in a Novakai pool.”

🏗️ Structure & Architecture

How the system is built — boxes, layers, tables, and the wires between them.

Class diagram (UML)

Boxes with name / fields / methods, linked by inheritance and association lines.

Ask for it when… you're designing or documenting a data model in code.

Say to Kimi: “UML class diagram of Agent, Mission, and Task with their key fields and relationships.”

Component / architecture diagram

Big boxes for systems or services, arrows for how they talk. The classic whiteboard picture.

Ask for it when… someone new needs the whole system in one picture.

Say to Kimi: “Architecture diagram of Novakai: app on 3030, backend on 3031, worker PTYs, and the JSONL stores.”

ER diagram

Entities as tables with their fields, crow's-foot lines showing one-to-many relationships.

Ask for it when… you're designing the JSONL stores or a database schema.

Say to Kimi: “ER diagram of missions, tasks, and decisions with primary keys and the one-to-many links.”

Network diagram

Nodes and edges showing what's connected to what on a network or mesh.

Ask for it when… you want to see every running thing and what it can reach.

Say to Kimi: “Network diagram of my two lanes: live 3030/3031, dev 3130/3131, and the worker agents hanging off the live backend.”

Mind map

A central idea with branches radiating outward — great for brainstorming structure.

Ask for it when… you're at the fuzzy start of an idea and need branches, not order.

Say to Kimi: “Mind map of everything Novakai Command touches: agents, stores, missions, messaging.”

Org chart

Hierarchy of people or agents — who reports to whom, top-down boxes.

Ask for it when… you need to show reporting lines or the agent hierarchy at a glance.

Say to Kimi: “Org chart of the team: Chris → Chief → three Managers, with Workers under Manager A.”

📅 Time & Planning

When things happen — deadlines, phases, and the shape of a week or a quarter.

Timeline

A line with dated milestones — the story of what happened (or will) in order.

Ask for it when… you want the narrative of a project or incident in date order.

Say to Kimi: “Timeline of Novakai milestones from the orchestrator plan to today.”

Gantt chart

Horizontal bars: one row per task, bar spans its start→end dates. Shows overlap and duration.

Ask for it when… a mission has parallel tasks and you need to see who finishes when.

Say to Kimi: “Gantt chart of this mission: brief Mon, build Mon–Wed, review Wed–Thu, ship Thu, retro Fri.”

Roadmap (now / next / later)

Three columns of intent — no fake dates, just priority horizons. Novakai OKRs use exactly this.

Ask for it when… you want direction without committing to dates you don't believe in.

Say to Kimi: “Now/next/later roadmap for Novakai based on the current OKRs.”

Calendar heatmap

GitHub-style grid of days; darker = more activity. Spot streaks and dead zones instantly.

Ask for it when… you want a feel for rhythm and consistency over weeks.

Say to Kimi: “Calendar heatmap of agent activity across the stores for the last month.”

📈 Data & Quantity

Numbers with shape — comparisons, trends, distributions, and flows of volume.

Bar / line / pie — the classics

Bars compare categories, lines show trends over time, pies show share of a whole.

Ask for it when… you have plain numbers and just want the obvious chart — don't overthink it.

Say to Kimi: “Bar chart of open tasks per agent, and a line of missions completed per week.”

Scatter plot

Dots on an X/Y grid — reveals correlation (or lack of it) between two variables.

Ask for it when… you suspect two things move together and want proof.

Say to Kimi: “Scatter plot of brief length vs. mission completion time — is there a pattern?”

Histogram

Bars of binned ranges — shows the shape of a distribution, not categories.

Ask for it when… you want to know what's “normal” and what's an outlier in one number.

Say to Kimi: “Histogram of DM message lengths in the journal — where's the bulge?”

Heatmap (matrix)

A colored grid where cell intensity = value. Great for load-by-agent-by-day.

Ask for it when… “who's overloaded?” is a grid question — two axes plus intensity.

Say to Kimi: “Heatmap of messages handled per agent per weekday — who's hot?”

Sankey diagram

Flowing ribbons whose width = quantity, showing how a total splits and recombines.

Ask for it when… you want to follow a volume as it splits across paths.

Say to Kimi: “Sankey of incoming requests splitting into auto-handled vs. escalated-to-Chris.”

Funnel

Stacked shrinking bars — each stage keeps only some of the previous one. Where do things drop off?

Ask for it when… users/items pass through stages and you need the leakiest stage.

Say to Kimi: “Funnel from signup to weekly active — where are we losing people?”

🧮 Relationships & Comparison

Side-by-side thinking — overlaps, trade-offs, and structured showdowns.

Venn diagram

Overlapping circles showing what sets share and what they don't.

Ask for it when… two or three roles/tools overlap and the intersection is the interesting part.

Say to Kimi: “Venn of what Chief, Manager, and Worker can each do — highlight shared powers.”

2×2 matrix / quadrant

Two axes, four boxes — the classic prioritization tool (impact vs. effort, urgent vs. important).

Ask for it when… you need to sort a pile of ideas/tasks by two competing criteria.

Say to Kimi: “2×2 of these feature ideas by impact vs. effort — tell me the quick wins.”

Comparison table

Rows of criteria, columns of options, ✅/❌ in the cells. Boring name, unbeatable clarity.

WidgetMermaidScreenshot
Offline✅✅✅
Editable later✅✅❌
Interactive✅❌❌
Zero setup❌✅✅

Ask for it when… you're picking between tools, vendors, or approaches on fixed criteria.

Say to Kimi: “Comparison table of widget vs. mermaid vs. screenshot for the docs.”

Radar / spider chart

Axes radiating from a center; each series draws a polygon. Compares profiles across several dimensions.

Ask for it when… two options each have a “shape” across 4–6 qualities.

Say to Kimi: “Radar chart comparing approach A vs. B on speed, quality, cost, reach, fun.”

Treemap

Nested rectangles sized by value — see what dominates a whole (disk usage, budget, store sizes).

Ask for it when… you want to know what's eating the space/budget/attention.

Say to Kimi: “Treemap of .novakai/stores by file size — which store is the hog?”

👤 People & Experience

The human side — what someone feels, does, and hits along the way.

User journey map

Stages across the top; rows for actions, thoughts, and feelings (an emoji mood line).

Ask for it when… you want to find the moment users (or agents!) struggle or delight.

Say to Kimi: “Journey map of a new user's first week: discover, onboard, first win, habit — with a mood line.”

Persona card

A one-page profile of an archetypal user: goals, frustrations, quote, context.

Ask for it when… a feature needs a face — someone concrete to build for.

Say to Kimi: “Persona card for our target user: a solo founder orchestrating AI agents.”

Storyboard

Comic-strip panels showing a scenario frame by frame — context, action, outcome.

Ask for it when… you need to sell a scenario to humans, not specify it for machines.

Say to Kimi: “Three-panel storyboard: Chris writes a brief, Chief splits it, the work ships.”

🖥️ Product & Code

Software-flavored visuals — screens, dependencies, branches, and boards.

Wireframe / mockup

Low-fi boxes standing in for UI — layout before pixels, pixels before code.

Ask for it when… you want to agree on layout before anyone argues about colors.

Say to Kimi: “Wireframe the dashboard: sidebar nav, big chart, two stat cards below.”

Dependency graph

Directed graph of what depends on what — build order and circular-dependency hunting.

Ask for it when… a change keeps breaking things and you need to see the blast radius.

Say to Kimi: “Dependency graph of the scripts and backend — what breaks if I touch the stores?”

Git graph

Commits as dots on branching lines — where main and feature branches split and merge.

Ask for it when… branch strategy gets confusing and a picture ends the argument.

Say to Kimi: “Git graph of main plus the two team branches and where they merged back.”

Kanban board

Columns for status (todo / doing / done) with cards that move left to right.

Ask for it when… you want mission/task status you can read from across the room.

Say to Kimi: “Kanban of the current mission's tasks from tasks.jsonl — todo, doing, done.”
